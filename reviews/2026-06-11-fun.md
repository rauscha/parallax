# Parallax — Fun Review

**Date:** 2026-06-11 · **Branch:** `claude/codebase-shipping-review-q5whpr` · **Lens:** grin-factor. How cool, delightful, and joy-inducing is this thing?

**Method:** code-only. I read the delight machinery (haptics, detents, surprise, scope, postcard, themes, copy) and traced the interaction paths. I cannot run a browser, so anything that needs hands-on play to confirm is explicitly tagged **[needs play-test]**.

---

## 1. Verdict

**Fun grade: B+** — *a beautifully-built instrument with a slightly shy front door.*

One-liner: **Parallax is a Stradivarius in a room where the lights come on one switch at a time.** The engine room is an A: real Eurorack DSP, 80 hand-annotated synthesis models, knobs that physically *click* home, an oscilloscope that breathes when idle and blooms when you play. The first sixty seconds are a C+: you tap in, hear one polite A440 ping, and then face an empty sequencer with a disabled play button. Almost every joy this app contains is *opt-in*, and the single most fun button in the product ("Surprise me") is a small pill in the corner — and on phones it's literally inside a `⋯` junk drawer. The toys are world-class; the toy store keeps them behind the counter.

## 2. Delight inventory — land or miss

Each deliberate fun-engineering decision I found, with a call: **LANDS** (genuine joy), **GIMMICK** (decorative, low payoff), or **MISS** (intent good, execution undercuts it).

### 2.1 Magnetic knob detent with haptic catch — **LANDS** (the best single interaction in the app)
`src/ui/Knob.svelte:17-28, 99-110`. This is real instrument-feel engineering, not a tween: catch zone ±2.5% of range, *break-away* at ±5% (hysteresis so it can't chatter), a haptic tick **only on catch** (`Knob.svelte:105`), and a guard so grabbing a knob already at default doesn't fire a spurious buzz (`Knob.svelte:118-121`). The detent tick mark on the dial *lights up in the signal colour while you're caught in it* (`Knob.svelte:213, 274-277`). For bipolar FM this is the classic centre detent. This is the kind of detail Teenage Engineering charges $1,400 for. **[needs play-test]** only for the exact stickiness feel (5% of the ±12-semitone FM range = ~1.2 semitones of break-away travel — could feel gluey; judgment call on hardware).

### 2.2 Note-entry preview: hear + feel the pitch you place — **LANDS**
`src/notation/GridEditor.svelte:124-147`. Tap a grid cell → haptic tick (6ms) + a 220ms audible preview of that exact pitch at velocity 0.7, with the still-ringing previous preview released first (mono-voice hygiene, `:139`). Cleverest part: the preview is **suppressed during playback** because a preview would steal the mono voice and glitch the loop (`:127, :135`) — that's joy-protection, not joy-omission. Keyboard placement (`Space`) also previews (`:469`). This makes melody entry feel like plinking a kalimba instead of filling in a spreadsheet.

### 2.3 "Surprise me" — **LANDS**, and it's the secret protagonist
`src/state/surprise.ts`. A genuine slot machine: random engine (which **flips the whole theme** — costume change included in the price of the spin), random model from up to 80, randomized params, fresh in-key melody in a random key/scale. The randomizer is *musically chaperoned*: gain clamped 0.3–0.6 so a roll is never silent or blasting (`surprise.ts:31-33`), attack/release/drift clamped away from the unpleasant extremes — the comment literally says "so a roll stays fun" (`surprise.ts:43-46`). The button does the dice-emoji bit: `⚄ Rolling…` while in flight (`App.svelte:65`). The theme-flip side effect is the masterstroke — every spin is also a set change.

**The miss inside the land:** the roll always replaces your melody too. There's a `swapEngine: false` escape hatch in the API (`surprise.ts:56-57`) but the UI exposes only the full nuke. No "reroll the sound, keep my tune" — the move you want after you've made something you like.

### 2.4 Oscilloscope character pack — **LANDS** (and answers the old review's harshest visual criticism)
`src/viz/Oscilloscope.svelte`. The 2026-05-31 deep review called the resting scope "a dead flat line." It's now the most alive surface in the app:

- **Idle breathing**: alpha-pulsing baseline on a 4-second sine (`:134-147`) plus a faint beam dot sweeping left→right every 6 seconds (`:149-158`) — "a real scope at rest isn't dead, it pulses softly." Both gated on `prefers-reduced-motion`. Chef's kiss.
- **Boot sweep**: a one-shot bright vertical scan timed to outlast the A440 confirmation strike ("the scope 'lights up' rather than blips," `:14-23, 196-215`). The app's first second has choreography.
- **Per-theme bloom as design tokens**: `--scope-bloom` 0.45 (crisp SNES vector) / 1.0 (warm Sandbox) / 1.7 (heavy Phosphor CRT), `--scope-persist` 0.85/0.78/0.92 (`tokens.css:42-43, 101-102, 141-142`), real `shadowBlur` bloom gated on amplitude (`:183-189`). The scope literally has a different *personality* per theme, driven by CSS custom properties. This is the correct, coolest way to do it.
- **Phosphor-only CRT vignette + scanlines** (`Oscilloscope.svelte:260-268`, `tokens.css:159-171`) — curved-glass falloff sold with one radial-gradient.

### 2.5 "Show me" macro-knob sweep — **LANDS** (the teaching moment as theatre)
`src/state/show-me.ts`. Press a button on an Explain card and the knob *physically animates* through original → min → max → original over 2.6s while you hear it, with the middle leg deliberately the longest because "that's the reveal" (`show-me.ts:66-73` — an eased dramaturgy comment!). If nothing's sounding it holds its own middle-C demo note; if you're already playing it rides your audio (`:160-164`). Interrupt handling is exhaustive: second click, grabbing any knob, model/engine swap, tab-hide — all bail cleanly and restore (`:110-135`). This converts the manual into a magic trick.

### 2.6 Model-change pulse — **LANDS** (small, correct)
`src/ui/ModelPicker.svelte:233-242`. The code pill does a 320ms overshoot pop on every model change — with the design rationale in the comment: "The whole point of Braids is cycling through models, so the act of changing one earns a beat." Skips the mount flash (`:54-59`), respects reduced motion. Tiny, but it's the right instinct: celebrate the core verb.

### 2.7 The writing — **LANDS**, genuinely
The model lore is the best copy in the app. `braids-models.ts` reads like a friend at a modular meet: WTFM's "park it right at the edge before it breaks up for the sweet spot" (`:273`), SYN-W "sequence or modulate COLOR and it almost talks" (`:121`), TOY\* "when you want it to feel charmingly busted" (`:185`), WMAP "there's no wrong setting, only territory to explore" (`:361`). Laxsynth keeps the voice: OVFL's "SIZE and MULT make it more or less unhinged" (`laxsynth-models.ts:79`), and TRI's Mirror knob honestly admits "Does almost nothing here… Use it on PULSE for real PWM" (`:43`) — an explain panel that tells you a knob is useless is an explain panel you *trust*. Search placeholder: "Search models — try 'bell', 'fm', 'noise'…" (`ModelPicker.svelte:179`). Splash fine print: "Browsers require a click before they'll make sound. One tap and you're in." (`TapToStart.svelte:54-56`). Friendly, concrete, zero corporate filler.

### 2.8 Postcard — **LANDS** as craft, **MISS** as a loop (see §6)
`src/ui/postcard.ts` draws a real 1200×630 social card in the *live theme's colours*: wordmark, engine·model headline at 76px, actual macro-dial states, a mini piano-roll of your melody, tempo/key footer (`postcard.ts:180-248`). Beautiful and deterministic. But the PNG contains **no share URL** — only the bare domain (`:244`). The image shows your friend a sound they cannot click to hear. The two halves of the share loop (postcard image, `#p=` link) are separate buttons that don't know about each other (`PatchToolbar.svelte:44-56`).

### 2.9 Share URLs — **LANDS**
`src/state/share-url.ts`. The whole instrument — engine, model, every knob, full melody — round-trips through `location.hash` via lz-string; nothing touches a server (`:1-15`). Button gives "Copied!" / graceful "In address bar" fallback (`PatchToolbar.svelte:26-38`). Share-link boot path reads the hash *before* starting the default engine so you land directly in the sender's engine + theme (`TapToStart.svelte:17-24`) — your friend's cursed patch arrives wearing its own costume.

### 2.10 Theme-follows-engine — **LANDS** (bold and coherent)
`src/state/theme.ts`. No theme menu: the voice *is* the skin. Braids=phosphor, Plaits=sandbox, Laxsynth=lab, with the mobile status-bar `theme-color` kept in step (`:17-22, 32`). It turns engine switching — usually a boring dropdown — into walking between three rooms. Surprise-me exploits it for free.

### 2.11 Grain texture — **GIMMICK** (pleasant, invisible-by-design)
`base.css:101-116`: fractal-noise tile at 4.5% opacity, overlay blend, with Phosphor opting out ("its scanlines are already the texture") and phones dropping it. Thoughtful; nobody will ever consciously notice it. That's fine — texture is supposed to be felt, not seen.

### 2.12 Haptics module — **LANDS** in concept, thin in coverage
`src/ui/haptics.ts` is 13 honest lines (Vibration API, silent no-op on iOS/desktop). Call sites: knob detent catch and grid note placement. **Not** called from the mobile NoteStrip chips (`NoteStrip.svelte` has no `hapticTick` import) — the one surface a phone user actually *plays* has no tactile feedback while the editing grid does. Inconsistent tactile story; see anti-fun §7.

### 2.13 Easter eggs — **absent**
I searched (`konami|easter|secret|…`): the only hit is the word "fun" in a surprise.ts comment. No konami code, no secret 48th model, no long-press-the-logo gag. For a project with a commit log this playful, the absence of a single hidden wink is a missed cheap thrill.

## 3. The first 60 seconds — traced

**0:00** — Splash: "Parallax / A web playground for the sounds of Braids" + one CTA (`TapToStart.svelte:46-50`). Clean, honest, fast. Good.

**0:02 (tap)** — `start()`: WASM engine boots, then a deliberate **confirmation strike** — A440 at velocity 0.5 for 260ms (`TapToStart.svelte:33-35`) — while the scope fires its 700ms boot sweep so "the visual energy lines up with the first sound" (`Oscilloscope.svelte:14-17`). **There IS a "whoa" at ~2 seconds** — a real synthesized ping with a CRT light-up, in phosphor green. It's small but it's real, and the WASM-failure path now rejects loudly with a human error instead of hanging silent (`BraidsEngine.ts:104-123` — the old P0 is fixed).

**0:03 onward — the cliff.** What greets you:
- Sequencer: **empty**. Footer: "Tap to place a note · drag right to extend" + a "Load demo" button (`App.svelte:140-143`).
- Transport: **▶ PLAY is disabled** (`App.svelte:159` — `disabled={!ready || eventCount === 0}`).
- The synth itself: CSAW at default timbre/color, silent until *you* discover the QWERTY map (desktop: a small caption box at the bottom of the controls column, `KeyboardHarness.svelte:108-114`) or the NoteStrip (mobile).
- "Load demo" plays… an **ascending C-major scale, quarter notes, two bars then silence** (`demo.ts:9-17` — the file's own comment says "TEMPORARY… Remove once the click-to-place staff editor lands," which happened milestones ago). The first melody a stranger hears from an app containing 808 kicks, vowel synthesis, and chaotic feedback FM is… a school scale exercise.

**The fastest grin path** is Tap → "⚄ Surprise me" → ▶ — three clicks to a random instrument in a random theme playing a random tune. But Surprise is a small top-right pill on desktop and **hidden behind the `⋯` disclosure on phones** (`ToolsMenu.svelte` collapses the whole cluster ≤720px). Nothing in the first-run experience points at it.

**Toy test verdict:** a knowledgeable visitor grins in 15 seconds. A zero-knowledge visitor gets one ping and then a quiet, handsome lab. Chrome Music Lab would have had them making noise *by accident* already. The gap is not capability — it's choreography. The app never offers its own first move.

## 4. Depth & discovery

**The toybox is genuinely deep — 47 Braids + 24 Plaits + 9 Laxsynth = 80 models — and the app mostly does right by it:**

- **Browsable, not buried**: the model picker is a permanent panel, not a dropdown — family-grouped list with sticky headers, search across code/name/family/description, ◀▶ steppers for adjacent flipping, keyboard cursor with arrows/Enter/Escape (`ModelPicker.svelte:65-150`). The hardware-style 4-char code pill with `NN / 46` counter scratches the Eurorack itch.
- **The Explain panel is lore done right**: per-model description + "Listen for" / "Good for" + per-knob cards with live % readouts + the "Show me" sweep (`ExplainPanel.svelte:69-122`). Knob↔card cross-highlighting (`Knob.svelte:30-48`) means touching a knob lights up its documentation — discovery is bidirectional.
- **Surprise as the depth-sampler**: forced engine swap (`surprise.ts:59-63`) means repeated rolls *tour* all three engines and all three themes. It's a slot machine whose jackpot is "you found CLOU and now you like granular synthesis."

**Where depth stays hidden:**
- The desktop model list is a **160px-tall peephole** (`ModelPicker.svelte:307`) onto an 80-model collection — roughly 5 rows visible. The family structure (Drums! Formants! Noise/Granular!) never gets to flex visually. There's no moment where the app shows you the *size* of the toybox.
- No per-model audio identity until you play: stepping models in silence is reading a menu with no smells. A model-change could borrow the note-preview trick (one short strike on model select) and the steppers would become an audition flipbook. **[needs play-test to confirm current feel]**
- The 28 lo-fi/envelope/FM params flow into shared rows (`ParamPanel`, commit #13) — workmanlike; the lo-fi bank (BITS/RATE/SIGN/DRIFT — actual Braids crust controls!) gets no special "danger zone" framing, which is a personality opportunity skipped.

## 5. Theme personality report cards

The schema is one set of tokens, three palettes (`tokens.css:1-5`) — and one theme commits much harder than the others.

### Phosphor (Braids) — **A−** · *"found Cold-War lab equipment"*
Fully commits. Near-black amber-tinted body (`#0A0805`), phosphor green `#7DFF6B`, **everything** set in JetBrains Mono including headings (`tokens.css:149-150`), uppercase labels with wide tracking, the heaviest scope persistence (0.92) and bloom (1.7), scanlines layer (`tokens.css:159-171`), CRT vignette on the scope glass (`Oscilloscope.svelte:262-268`), amber as the secondary signal. The fiction holds: this reads as an instrument you found in a decommissioned bunker. Only break: the modern rounded-rect buttons and the friendly sans-serif-style layout grid are 2026, not 1962 — forgivable.

### Sandbox (Plaits) — **B** · *"TE office, casual Friday"*
Warm putty body, hot orange `#FF5A1F`, charcoal-as-accent, **lowercase labels** ("TE lowercase aesthetic," `tokens.css:109`), dark inset scope screen on a bright body — the one genuinely skeuomorphic move (a dark screen in a light device, like an OP-1). Good palette discipline. But the bit mostly *is* the palette: no TE-style pictogram quirk, no playful unit readouts, same fonts as Lab. It gestures at Teenage Engineering without telling a TE joke.

### Lab Instrument (Laxsynth, SNES-inspired) — **B−** · *"the console is grey, where's the cartridge?"*
Neutral grey-lavender body, teal signal, warm gold accent, crisp low-bloom scope (0.45 — "crisp vector trace," `tokens.css:43`). Tasteful — *too* tasteful. The SNES inspiration reads as "muted grey with purple undertones" (accurate to the console shell!) but nothing here is *Nintendo*: no chunky pill buttons, no four-color wink (the SNES logo had one ready-made), and `--t-spring` — the bouncy overshoot easing token that would be this theme's signature motion — is defined (`tokens.css:65`) and **used nowhere** (the model-pill pop hardcodes its own curve). The Laxsynth engine has the most chiptune-adjacent content (PULS "the chip-lead staple," LFSR "the metallic NES / Game Boy noise channel" — `laxsynth-models.ts:69, 110`) and the theme doesn't capitalize on that kinship. The fiction breaks because it's understated to the point of anonymity.

**Cross-theme note:** because layout, components, and (except Phosphor) fonts are identical, the themes are three *liveries* on one aircraft. Phosphor escapes into personality because it changes typography + adds physical artifacts (scanlines, vignette, heavy persistence). The other two would each need one or two such signature artifacts to "commit to the bit."

## 6. Shareability-as-fun

The raw materials for "send your friend a cursed patch" are **all present and individually excellent**:

- **The cursed patch generator exists**: Surprise me will happily roll WTFM chaotic feedback FM in Db pentatonic at some absurd tempo. The gain/attack clamps (`surprise.ts:31-46`) keep "cursed" inside "playable" — correctly, since a *silent* cursed patch is no patch at all.
- **The delivery vehicle exists**: a `#p=` link is the complete instrument, opens in the right engine and theme (`TapToStart.svelte:17-32`), no server, no account.
- **The flex artifact exists**: a theme-colored postcard with your actual dial positions and melody.

What's missing is the **stitching**:
1. Postcard PNG has no link on/with it (§2.8) — the image and the URL are two different buttons (`PatchToolbar.svelte:44-56`) and nothing suggests sending them together. One "Copy card + link" action (or QR/link line baked into the PNG, or `navigator.share` with both) closes the loop.
2. **No Web Share API anywhere** — on mobile, where this loop would live (group chats), sharing means clipboard-then-switch-apps, and the buttons are behind `⋯`.
3. No reciprocity hook on arrival: opening a friend's link drops you into their patch — great — but there's no "now remix it and send it back" nudge (even just auto-highlighting Surprise/Share for link-arrivals).

Verdict: **the makings, yes; the loop, not yet.** Currently a postcard you mail in two envelopes.

## 7. Anti-fun audit — ranked by joy-damage

1. **The empty first stage** (highest damage). Empty sequencer + disabled PLAY + opt-in everything (§3). The app's own best demo of itself — Surprise — is never offered. *One* auto-loaded charming default melody (in-key, syncopated, not a scale) or a first-run "Roll me something" callout would convert the C+ opening into an A. (`App.svelte:140-143, 159`; `demo.ts`.)
2. **The demo melody is homework.** An ascending C-major scale (`demo.ts:10`) followed by two bars of silence. It's labeled TEMPORARY in its own docstring and has outlived three milestones. With 80 models of lore-rich character, the demo tune is the single worst writing in the product.
3. **Surprise demoted on mobile.** The hero delight lives behind `⋯` (`ToolsMenu.svelte`, ≤720px collapse). Phones are where a one-thumb slot machine shines most. The collapse logic is sound for Match/MIDI/Presets — Surprise deserves an exemption.
4. **Surprise always eats your melody** (`surprise.ts:82-86`). After you've sequenced something you love, the dice become dangerous instead of fun — the classic "I don't press the fun button anymore" failure. A "keep melody" variant (the API half-exists, `:56-57`) or a one-level undo would fix it.
5. **No haptic on the NoteStrip** (`NoteStrip.svelte` — zero `hapticTick` calls) while the *editing* grid buzzes (`GridEditor.svelte:134`). The phone play-surface is the least tactile surface in the app. Backwards.
6. **Randomized melodies are rhythmically square**: always quarter notes on the beat, ~30% rests (`grid.ts:138-145`). Every roll has the same gait; after five spins the melodies blur together while the sounds stay surprising. Even one eighth-note pair or a held half-note per bar would put swagger in the dice.
7. **Model browsing is silent** (§4) — flipping through the 80-model collection makes no sound until you separately play a note. **[needs play-test]** — if users naturally hold a key while stepping, this is moot; the code offers no nudge to do so.
8. **160px list viewport** (`ModelPicker.svelte:307`) hides the toybox's scale (§4).
9. *(Cleared from last review, worth crediting: the silent-WASM-hang P0 is fixed with a loud 10s timeout — `BraidsEngine.ts:122-123` — and stuck-note-on-blur is defended in both harnesses — `KeyboardHarness.svelte:78-92`, `NoteStrip.svelte:103-113`. Two former joy-killers, dead.)*

## 8. Benchmark positioning

| Benchmark | Their superpower | vs. Parallax |
|---|---|---|
| **Chrome Music Lab** | Sound within 2s of page load, zero literacy required, the *entire screen* is the instrument | CML wins minutes 0–1 decisively; Parallax wins minute 5 onward — CML has no depth floor below it, Parallax has 80 models of basement. |
| **pink.trombone** | One grotesque, irresistible direct-manipulation toy; instant social currency | Parallax literally *contains* pink.trombone's domain (VOWL/VFOF/VOSM, `braids-models.ts:219-241`) — as items 21–23 in a list. p.t. ships one organ; Parallax ships an anatomy textbook with the organ on p. 22. |
| **patatap** | Every keypress = sound + full-screen visual burst; performance toy | Parallax's keypress gives sound + scope wiggle but no per-note visual celebration. patatap feels like fireworks; Parallax feels like instrumentation. |
| **TE web toys (OP-1 field demos, etc.)** | Total vibe commitment; hardware fetish object as UI | Parallax's detent knob + theme-driven scope character are genuinely in this league (§2.1, §2.4); Sandbox theme tips the hat but doesn't match TE's joke density. |

**Position: upper-middle, climbing.** Parallax is the *deepest* thing in this lineup by a wide margin and has top-tier interaction craft (detent, Show-me sweep, scope character). What separates it from the top shelf is entirely in the first 60 seconds and the social loop — the two places where Chrome Music Lab and pink.trombone, with 5% of Parallax's substance, beat it on grins-per-visitor. The gap is closable with: an opening act (auto-demo or first-run Surprise), a promoted dice button, one undo, and a postcard that carries its own link.
